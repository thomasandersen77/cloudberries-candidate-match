CV Rafael Sanchez, PhD Cloudberries 2it offers quantifiable guarantees related to the risk of re-identification, and

Rafael recognized the complexity of generating synthetic versions of relational 
databases, a known challenge in the field. After a thorough assessment of the 
available solutions, he identified a method described in a research article as the 
most suitable. This method employed a differentially private multi-table 
synthesis algorithm, designed to link independently generated synthetic tables 
from single-table synthesis tools, by linking tables in pairs, while preserving 
statistical patterns and data distributions across tables. 
The chosen algorithm used marginal queries across tables and minimized a 
loss function to ensure good quality in relation to sharp data. Crucially, this 
approach enabled a modular approach, breaking down the complex problem of 
synthetic database generation into manageable steps. 
To ensure that all generated synthetic data was of sufficient quality, and that 
the privacy of the training data was actually safeguarded, Rafael devised a set 
of metrics that are calculated automatically upon synthesis.
The quality of the synthetic data was measured using metrics such as fidelity, 
utility and integrity. These quantified, among other things, how well the data 
distributions in the real data were recreated in the synthetic data.
Privacy metrics such as Distance to Closest Record and Membership Inference 
Attack measured how well the privacy framework – differential privacy – 
protected the training data.
